This booklet from 1898 is an early plea for birth-control. The name of Thomas Robert Malthus may now be much forgotten, but in 1798 (a century before this booklet), he anonymously published An Essay on the Principle of Population, in which he promotes celibacy and a delay in getting married to limit population growth.

This booklet goes further in that it recognizes the evils of celibacy, then continues to give a lot of detail on the struggle to get this type of information out. The actual (and very limited) information on practical means of anti-conception is finally found in the last few pages.
